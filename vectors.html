The causal test. We steer a liberal run along one topic's partisan direction and measure the shift toward conservative answers on every topic's items. Rows are the steering direction, columns the items being tested; a dark diagonal with light off-diagonal would mean separate per-topic knowledge; a uniformly dark matrix means one shared causal axis.

All directions are residual-stream differences of persona-conditioned activations at the final prompt token. Answer-relevant fraction = norm fraction of a direction inside the centered span of the option-letter unembeddings. Steering = adding c·direction to the output of a decoder layer during a single forward pass, liberal source persona, exact option-probability readout. Human benchmark: weighted inter-item correlations, GSS 2014–2024. Code: mech_interp_scripts/m_track/vector_*.py in the llm-liar repo.
