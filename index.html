The political middle: humans vs language models

Are self-identified moderates genuine centrists (Fowler et al. 2023) or cross-pressured people whose offsetting extremes only average to the middle (Broockman & Lauderdale 2025)? Below: 18,637 GSS respondents (2014–2024) and four open-weight language models, measured on the same 14 attitude items with the same ruler.

2 · Real moderates, General Social Survey 2014–2024

Three actual (anonymous) respondents who called themselves moderate, and the average over all of them. 65% of real moderates hold at least one extreme-liberal and one extreme-conservative position.

Pick a model and an item: gray bars are the 6,628 real moderates' answers; purple bars are the model's "moderate" persona (exact response probabilities, order-averaged). Below, the model's 7-point persona ladder against the 7 real ideology groups on the same axis.

4 · The measurement artifact worth knowing about

Reversing the on-screen order of the answer options shifts every model's content answer — often by more than half the scale. Bars show the size of that shift per item for the selected model and persona. Any silicon survey that does not randomize option order is measuring letter preference, not politics.

Near-middle response share of each middle. The models sit well above the human reference line: they portray the Fowlerian average of a Broockman-shaped public.

And the estimator test: a surrogate of the Fowler et al. mixture model was run on synthetic worlds with known ground truth, on the real GSS, and on silicon samples. Its verdict (y) barely moves while the truth (x) varies — and silicon middles sit on the Fowler side of the human point no matter what the human truth is.

Data: GSS cumulative file 2014–2024 (weighted; 14 ordinal items, empirically oriented so 0 = liberal end). Models elicited in completion format with exact option-probability readout, 2 frames × 2 option orders per cell; positions are order-averaged content positions. Cross-pressured = at least one position ≤ 0.15 and one ≥ 0.85 across ≥ 5 answered items; near-middle = |position − 0.5| < 0.17. Estimator: 3-class mixture (graded-response ideologue / shared-multinomial non-ideologue / uniform). Code: mech_interp_scripts/m_track/ and dashboard/build_data.py in the llm-liar repository.
